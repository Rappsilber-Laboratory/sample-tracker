This set of questions concerns the experimental conditions of crosslinking reactions. Such reactions occur in solution and are carried out by mixing protein samples with crosslinkers. To document the reaction conditions, it is important to record the following information:

Protein or Cell Concentration (number)
Protein or Cell Concentration Unit (unit)

{{ render_field(form.protein_or_cell_concentration) }}

{{ render_field(form.protein_or_cell_concentration_unit) }}

These two fields define the concentration of proteins or cells in a crosslinking reaction and should be entered as a numerical value and a corresponding unit. For isolated protein samples, the concentration is typically measured as either a molar concentration (e.g., mM) or a weight concentration (e.g., mg/mL). For in vivo crosslinking experiments, the concentration may instead be recorded as cell count per volume (e.g., 1 × 10⁶ cells/mL). Crosslinker or Compound Concentration
Crosslinker or Compound Concentration Unit

{{ render_field(form.crosslinker_or_compound_concentration) }}

{{ render_field(form.crosslinker_or_compound_concentration_unit) }}

These two fields define the concentration of the crosslinker or another chemical compound in the crosslinking reaction. Note that the concentration recorded here should be the final concentration in the reaction mixture, not the concentration of the stock solution. The value should be entered as a numerical value together with a unit. Concentrations are typically measured as molar concentrations (e.g., mM) or weight concentrations (e.g., mg/mL). Organic Solvent Concentration
Organic Solvent Concentration Unit

{{ render_field(form.organic_solvent_concentration) }}

{{ render_field(form.organic_solvent_concentration_unit) }}

Crosslinker stock solutions are sometimes prepared in organic solvents before being added to the crosslinking reaction. The final concentration of the organic solvent in the reaction should therefore also be recorded, as it may affect protein structure and may be important for interpreting the crosslinking data. This value can be entered as a numerical value together with a unit. In some cases, the concentration may be expressed as a molar concentration (e.g., 5 µM). More commonly, however, it is reported as a fraction of the total reaction volume (v/v), such as 2%. In the latter case, the unit field should be left empty.
